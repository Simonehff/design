Website redesign - Company repositioning

ENGIE Brasil

OVERVIEW

ENGIE Brazil is the biggest private energy company from Brazil. The website redesign, besides France Headquarters new guidelines, was also due a new market positioning. Developed by the Mint Interactive Lab.

The two designers of the team, myself and Fabio Dudas, were responsible for renewing the website information architecture, structurate the new styleguide, high fidelity mobile and desktop layout, prototypes with user flows and animation examples, Jira documentation and interface QA testing.

SERVICES

UI Design
Website Design

The company market positioning change was followed by architecture changes such as a new section named solutions. The solutions area gather all ENGIE's products and services, distributed by expertises linked with business segments - such as industry, agribusiness, health, cities and others.

In this project, I was mainly tasked with creating new UI components and adapt existent ones in order to best represent the client's market positioning.

Although the Headquarters website in France already gave us the guidelines of the new visual identity, the Brazil's website is substantially bigger and diverse in content, so we were given a free hand to propose new UI approaches as needed.

Alongside the design system update, I've been tasked with sorting out new UI design solutions based on the HQ's guidelines, new components interactions (such as hover and loading animations) and the high fidelity prototype. The website ended up with around a hundred figma desktop and mobile pages prototyped and animated.

The new ENGIE Brazil website also counts with darkmode, page reader and a brazilian sign language interpreter plugin.
